         KEYWORDS
         comparative effectiveness, methods, real-world data, safety, study design

         Key Points

         · We present two constructs designed to protect the integrity of the results of real-world data
            analyses: staging and clean room

         · Staging is a process of reviewing results from sequential preliminary analyses and evaluating
            the population size available and potential bias before committing to conducting the compar-
            ative analysis.

         · A clean room process is designed to protect the integrity of a study by allowing decisions
            about model specification changes and other protocol deviations to be made without knowl-
            edge of how they might affect subsequent analyses.

         · A clean room imposes restricted access to data and preliminary study results; it also includes
            policies governing exploratory analyses and protocol deviations, and audit/decision logs.

         · An example for implementing staging with a clean room is provided.

         Plain Language Summary

         There are concerns that data analyses can be tilted in the direction of desired results and that
         only selected results are reported. We describe two approaches designed to increase confidence
         that the results from analyses of data from nonrandomized studies are valid: staging and clean
         room. Staging is a multi-step process during, which results from preliminary analyses are
         reviewed. There can be check points after each step during which a review team can evaluate
         whether the data are appropriate for conducting a comparative analysis. For example, the
         review team can evaluate if the study size is large enough to be informative or if the treatment
         groups are comparable. Depending on the results of these initial analyses, at each check point,
         the review team can recommend to stop the analysis, continue to the next step, or make modifi-
         cations to the study protocol. If modifications to the protocol occur, they should be made before
         starting a formal comparison of the treatments. A clean room restricts access to data and to pre-
         liminary study results, allowing for exploratory analyses to be conducted, while limiting knowl-
         edge for how these will affect the results of subsequent comparative safety and effectiveness
         analyses.
